Science & Methodology

Understanding how we turn neurodivergent friction into fluid momentum.

The Cognitive Gap

For individuals with ADHD, the transition from "intention" to "action" is often blocked by executive dysfunction. This is known as The Wall of Awful—a psychological barrier built from past failures, shame, and the sheer overwhelming nature of complex tasks.

Similarly, for Dyslexic users, traditional task managers increase Cognitive Load by presenting dense, cluttered interfaces that force the brain to work harder just to read the goal, let alone start it.

Our 4-Step Atomization Process

01

The Brain Dump (Input)

We provide a low-friction entry point. Don't worry about grammar, order, or priority. Just get the overwhelming thought out of your working memory and onto the screen.

02

Micro-Chunking (The Slice)

Our algorithm identifies "frictional nouns" and "vague verbs." If a task is "Clean the Kitchen," we slice it until the first step is as small as "Pick up one glass." This bypasses the Amygdala's fight-or-flight response to big projects.

03

Visual Anchoring (Scaffolding)

We present these steps in a vertical, single-focus stream. Using the Lexend font family and increased letter spacing, we ensure that Dyslexic eyes can track text without line-skipping or letter-swapping.

04

Dopamine-Positive Loops

Completion of "micro-tasks" provides frequent hits of dopamine. For ADHD brains, this consistent feedback loop is essential for maintaining the momentum required to finish the larger goal.

Why it works for Dyslexia:

Our interface avoids "Visual Crowding." By utilizing white space and a typeface designed for reading proficiency, we reduce the energy spent on decoding, allowing that energy to be spent on doing.

Built on Evidence

Our methodology integrates principles from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Universal Design for Learning (UDL). We don't just organize tasks; we organize the way you perceive them.
